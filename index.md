# Privacy Policy for CrimVault

**Last updated: April 18, 2026**

CrimVault is a Criminology Licensure Examination review app. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app stores, how it is used, and what choices users have.

This policy is based on the current app source code. It is provided for app publication and user transparency, but it is not legal advice.

## 1. Information the App Stores

CrimVault is designed to keep review data on the user's device. The app does not include a custom backend server in the current repository.

The app may store the following information locally:

- Account profile details, such as username, full name, school, selected avatar, profile image path, and exam date.
- Login security data, including password salt and password hash stored with secure device storage. The app does not store the plain password.
- Study progress, including answered questions, correct answers, XP, streaks, studied dates, activity logs, mock exam history, bookmarks, wrong-answer counts, and readiness data.
- Flashcard review data, including review intervals, next review dates, and daily swipe counts.
- App settings, including text size and onboarding flags.
- Premium or trial status, including local entitlement and trial-start data.

## 2. How the App Uses Information

CrimVault uses locally stored information to:

- Let users sign in to a local profile on the device.
- Save study progress and continue sessions.
- Track performance, readiness, streaks, XP, bookmarks, weak areas, and wrong answers.
- Show mock exam history and analytics.
- Schedule flashcard review using spaced repetition.
- Personalize the profile and exam countdown.
- Apply free, trial, and premium access limits.

## 3. Data Sharing

CrimVault does not sell personal information.

Based on the current repository, CrimVault does not send study progress, profile information, question answers, analytics, or profile photos to a developer-operated server.

Some third-party platform services may process data when users use platform features:

- **In-app purchases:** Purchases are handled by the app store billing provider, such as Google Play or Apple App Store depending on platform availability. CrimVault receives purchase status and product identifiers needed to unlock premium access. Payment card details are handled by the app store provider, not by CrimVault.
- **Fonts and assets:** The app uses bundled assets and disables runtime fetching for Google Fonts in the current source.

## 4. Device Permissions

CrimVault may request these permissions only when needed:

- **Camera:** Used when the user chooses to take a profile photo.
- **Photos or media library:** Used when the user chooses to select a profile image from the device.
- **Internet:** Used for platform services such as in-app purchase availability and purchase processing.

Profile photos are used for the in-app profile experience and are not uploaded to a CrimVault server in the current app implementation.

## 5. In-App Purchases

CrimVault may offer optional premium access, including products identified in the code as:

- `crimvault_elite_monthly`
- `crimvault_elite_lifetime`

Purchases, refunds, cancellations, and subscription management are handled by the applicable app store. Users should manage subscriptions through their app store account settings.

## 6. Data Retention and Deletion

Because CrimVault stores app data locally, most data remains on the device until the user removes it.

Users can delete or reset data by:

- Using in-app profile/settings actions such as reset progress, clear wrong answers, start fresh, delete account, or logout/switch user where available.
- Clearing app storage through the device settings.
- Uninstalling the app.

Some purchase records may remain with the app store provider according to that provider's own policies.

## 7. Security

CrimVault uses local device storage and secure storage APIs for sensitive local values such as password salts, password hashes, trial starts, and premium entitlement flags.

No method of storage is perfectly secure. Users should protect their device with a passcode, biometric lock, or other device security features.

## 8. Children's Privacy

CrimVault is intended for criminology students and board exam reviewers. It is not directed to children under 13. The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

If a parent or guardian believes a child has provided information in the app, they may delete the app data from the device or contact the developer.

## 9. Third-Party Links and Services

CrimVault may rely on app store billing services for purchases. Those services are governed by their own privacy policies and terms.
